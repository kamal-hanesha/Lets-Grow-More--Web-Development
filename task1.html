Sensors

  • A device which detects or measures a physical quantity and records,indicates or respond to it.

  • Broadcast definition: A sensor is a device,module or subsystem whose purpose

    is to detect events or changes in its environment and send the information to other electronics,frequently,

    a computer processer.It is always used with other electronics.

  • Sensor is a device or a module that helps to detect any changes in the physical quantities like pressure,

    temperature,humidity, force or electrical quantities like current or any other form of energy.After observing

    changes in physical quantities sensor sends information to the microprocessor or microcontroller.



Good Sensor :-

A good sensor obeys the following rules:

  1. It is sensitive to the measured property.

  2. It is insensitive to any other property likely to be encountered in its application.

  3. It doesnot influence the measured property




Criteria to choose a sensor:-

  1. Accuracy

  2. Enivironmental condition:-usually have limits for temperature or humidity.

  3. Range :-measurement limit of sensor.

  4. Calibaration:-essential for most of the measuring devices as the readings changes with time.

  5. Resolution:-small increment detected by sensor.

  6. Cost

  7. Reapeatability :-the reading that varies is reapeatedly measured under the same environment.

Basic sensors

  1. Electromagnetic sensors:-This type of sensors measure electrical resistance,current,voltage,power

    depending on specific device.This category also includes magnetic sensors.

  2. Mechanical sensors:-sensors that guage pressure,such as barometers,as well as gas and liquid flow sensors.

    viscosity and density sensors,humidity sensors ,vibration and shock sensors..etc.

  3. chemical sensors:-The household carbon-monoxide detector,oxygen sensors,odor sensors,redox and ion-selective

    electrodes are types of chemical sensors.

  4. Acoustic sensors:-It depends on ultrasound time-of-flight echo return-applications such as sonar and even certain

  5. Motion sensors:-The radaar guns that police use to track speeding are good examples of motion sensors.odometers and

Different types of sensors

Temperature sensor:-

This device collects information about the sensor and it converts into another form.

The best example to this device is mercury in thermometer.There are two types of temperature sensor.

1. Contact sensors:-It requires direct physical contact with the object being sensed

2.Non-contact sensors:-It doesnot requires direct physical contact with the object or media being sensed.

Applications:-Temperature sensors used everywhere like computers,mobile phones,automobiles, air conditioning

system,industries..etc



DHT22 sensor

The DHT22 is a basic, low-cost digital temperature and humidity sensor. It uses a capacitive

humidity sensor and a thermistor to measure the surrounding air, and spits out a digital

signal on the data pin (no analog input pins needed). It's fairly simple to use, but requires

careful timing to grab data.



Proximity sensor

A proximity sensor is a non-contact type sensor that detects the presence of an object. It can be implimented using different

techniques like infrared or laser,ultrasonic,hall effect,capacitive.

Applications:- Mobile phones,cars(parking sensors),industry(object alignment),ground proximity in aircrafts,

automation engineering,.etc.

Proximity sensors are non-contacting devices that measure the relative distance between the probe tip and the conductive surface

that is observing



Accelerometers & Gyroscope sensors

Accelerometer is a type of sensor that is used to detect changes in position,velocity and vibration by sensing

motion.It can be either analog type or digital type.In analog accelerometer,depending on volume of acceleration

applied to accelerometer,continuous analog voltage signal is produced.


Gyroscope sensor is to sense and determine the orientation with the help of Earth's gravity i.e it measures

the angular velocity.The main difference between Accelerometers and Gyroscope sensors is that Gyroscope can sense

the rotation where accelerometer can't In other words,Gyroscope measure any rotation and unaffected by acceleration

and accelerometer can't distinguish rotation from acceleration and unable to work when in center of rotation.



Current sensors

Current sensors helps us to detect electrical current,AC or DC,which is flowing through a wire.These sensors are

going to be able to pick up the amount of current that runs through the wire.We can find them as a part of Hall effect sensor.



Fiber optic sensors

Fiber optic sensor is a type of sensor that makes use of optical fibers.They can use these fibers in order to

relay signals from remote sensors to electronics or they can use to sense things.The relaying sensors are extrinsic sensors while

those that use the fiber optics for sensing are intrinsic sensors.

These sensors are used for measuring strain,temperature,pressure and other quantities



Touch screen sensors

Touch screen sensor is the most used sensor in mobile phones and tablets.They are able to determine when you are touching

the screen and the movements that your fingers make on the screen.



Wireless Motion sensors

Wireless motion sensor is able to detect objects that are moving,and then tell the device to take some type of action.

In most of the times, we use motion sensors to detect people,such as home security system.However,hunters and field

researchers utilze game cameras that have wireless motion sensors as well.



Ultrasonic Sensors

Ultrasonic sensors are used to measure distance or travel time using ultrasonic waves.A source will be used to emit

ultrasonic wave. After wave hits the target,the waves are reflected and the detector collects the signal.The travel time between the

transmitted wave and reflected wave is measured using ultrasonic sensor.Optical sensors use two different element for transmitter and

receiver whereas ultrasonic sensor uses single element for transmission and reception.

Applications of ultrasonic sensor:- measures the portion of the UV spectrum which sunburns human skin,pharmacy,automobiles,

robotics,chemical industries..etc.

Ultrasonic sensor with circuit can be used for measuring distance of an object.This method is used,where we cannot impliment the conventional methods

Infrared sensor

Infrared sensor(IR sensor): This device emits and/or detects infrared radiation tosense a particular phase

in the environment.Generally,thermal radiation is emitted by all the objects on the infrared spectrum.

It detects this type of radiation which is not visible to human eye.It is disturbed by noises in the sorroundings.

Applications:- It has various applications like proximity and object detection,used in mobile phones,industries,

street lights using IR sensor.IR detector is usually a photo diode.

T here are two types of IR sensors,transmittive type and reflective type.

Transmittive type IR sensor:- The IR transmitter and IR detector are positioned facing each other,object passes between them.

Reflective type IR sensor:- In this transmitter and detector are positioned adjacent to eac other facing the object.



Light sensors

These sensors are used to detect light.

Some of the devices that use light sensors are digital cameras,barcode scanners and more.



Gas sensor

A gas sensor or detector is able to determine the presence of a gas in a certain area.These types of sensors are often

useful for determining whether there is a gas leak of some type.Some sensors will even have alarms with them

that can sound if there is a gas leak and can help to pinpoint the area of leak.These types of sensors will often operate

via battery



HVAC Sensors

sensors for heating,ventilation and air conditioning systems can be quite important today.When you input the temperature that

you want for the system,the HVAC sensors will compare the state of the room or the building to the temperature that you want and

then adjust the system so that it is able to reach and maintain that desired temperature.



Advanced sensor technologies

sensor technologies are used in wide range in the field of manufacturing.The advanced technologies are as follows:-

  1. Bar-code Identification:- The products sold in the markets has a Universal Product code(UPC) which is a 12 digit code.

    Five of the numbers signify the manufacturer and other five signify the product.The first six digits are represented by code as a

    light and dark bars.The first digit signifies the type of number system and the second digit which is parity signifies the accuracy

    of the reading. The remaining six digits are represented by code as dark and light bars reversing the order of the first six digits.

    Bar code is shown in the figure given below.


    The bar code reader can manage different bar code standards even without having the knowledge of the standard code. The disadvantage with

    bar coding is that the bar scanner is unable to read if the bar code is concealed with grease or dirt.

  2. Transponders – In the automobile section, Radio frequency device is used in many cases. The transponders are hidden inside the plastic

    head of the key which is not visible to anyone. The key is inserted in the ignition lock cylinder. As you turn the key, the computer

    transmits a radio signal to the transponder. The computer will not let the engine to ignite until the transponder responds to the signal.

    These transponders are energized by the radio signals. The figure of a transponder is as shown below:

    A image of embedded transponder used in key.
  3. Electromagnetic Identification of Manufactured Components – This is similar to the bar code technology where the data can be coded on

    magnetic stripe. With magnetic striping, the data can be read even if the code is concealed with grease or dirt.

  4. surface Acoustic Waves :- This process is similar to the RF identification. Here, the part identification gets triggered by the

    radar type signals and is transmitted over long distances as compared to the RF systems.

  5. Optical Character Recognition :- This is a type of automatic identification technique which uses alphanumeric characters as the source

    of information. In United States, Optical character recognition is used in mail processing centres. They are also used in vision systems

    and voice recognition systems.
